Time & Space Complexity

What is the Time Complexity?
The time complexity of the backtracking algorithm used to solve Sudoku is exponential. At each step of the algorithm, a decision is made to place a number (from 1 to 9) in an empty cell. With a branching factor of up to 9 choices per cell and a depth of recursion depending on the number of empty cells, the time complexity is typically expressed as O(9m*n), where m is the number of empty cells and n is the number of decisions or steps required to solve the Sudoku.

What is the Space Complexity?
The space complexity primarily involves the recursive call stack. Each recursion depth corresponds to a decision point in placing numbers in the Sudoku grid. Therefore, the space complexity is O(m), where m is the maximum recursion depth. In practical terms, m can be up to 81-n, where n is the number of non-empty cells in the initial Sudoku grid.

All of this exponential growth in time complexity and the linear growth in space complexity are important considerations, especially for larger Sudoku grids or highly sparse initial configurations.
